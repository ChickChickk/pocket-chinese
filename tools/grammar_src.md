# Practical Chinese Grammar: 125 Essential Patterns

> Traditional Chinese · Taiwan-focused · Beginner to Intermediate

Each entry gives the function, reusable structure, natural example, translation, and a common warning.

## How to study

1. Learn the function rather than memorizing one English translation.
2. Read the example aloud.
3. Replace one part with your own vocabulary.
4. Review contrasting patterns together.

# 1. Sentence Foundations

## 1. 是

**Function:** identify or define.

**Structure:** `A + 是 + B`

> 我是印尼人。  
> I am Indonesian.

**Common warning:** Do not normally use 是 before ordinary adjectives.

## 2. 有

**Function:** express possession or existence.

**Structure:** `Subject/Place + 有 + Noun`

> 我有兩個妹妹。  
> I have two younger sisters.

**Common warning:** Use 沒有—not 不有—for the negative.

## 3. 在（location）

**Function:** show location.

**Structure:** `Subject + 在 + Place`

> 我的手機在桌上。  
> My phone is on the table.

**Common warning:** Do not add 是 in a neutral location sentence.

## 4. 很 + adjective

**Function:** form a natural adjective predicate.

**Structure:** `Subject + 很 + Adjective`

> 今天很冷。  
> It is cold today.

**Common warning:** 很 does not always carry strong emphasis.

## 5. 也

**Function:** add similar information.

**Structure:** `Subject + 也 + Predicate`

> 她會中文，我也會。  
> She speaks Chinese, and I do too.

**Common warning:** Place 也 before the verb or adjective.

## 6. 都

**Function:** refer to all members.

**Structure:** `Plural subject + 都 + Predicate`

> 我們都喜歡這家店。  
> We all like this shop.

**Common warning:** 都 comes after the subject.

## 7. 不

**Function:** negate habits, states, or future actions.

**Structure:** `Subject + 不 + Verb/Adjective`

> 我不喝咖啡。  
> I do not drink coffee.

**Common warning:** Do not normally use 不 for a completed past action.

## 8. 沒有 / 沒

**Function:** negate possession or completed actions.

**Structure:** `Subject + 沒(有) + Verb/Noun`

> 我今天沒有吃早餐。  
> I did not eat breakfast today.

**Common warning:** Use 沒有 for past non-completion.

## 9. 嗎

**Function:** form a yes–no question.

**Structure:** `Statement + 嗎？`

> 你明天有空嗎？  
> Are you free tomorrow?

**Common warning:** Do not combine 嗎 with a question word.

## 10. 呢

**Function:** ask a follow-up or soften a question.

**Structure:** `Topic + 呢？`

> 我很好，你呢？  
> I am fine. How about you?

**Common warning:** 呢 has no single fixed English translation.

## 11. 誰

**Function:** ask about a person.

**Structure:** `誰 + Predicate?`

> 門口的人是誰？  
> Who is the person at the door?

**Common warning:** Keep 誰 where the answer would appear.

## 12. 什麼

**Function:** ask about a thing.

**Structure:** `Subject + Verb + 什麼？`

> 你想吃什麼？  
> What do you want to eat?

**Common warning:** Do not move the question word to the front.

## 13. 哪 / 哪個

**Function:** ask which one.

**Structure:** `哪 + Measure word + Noun?`

> 你要哪一杯？  
> Which cup do you want?

**Common warning:** 哪 usually needs a measure word before a noun.

## 14. 哪裡

**Function:** ask about a place.

**Structure:** `Subject + 在/去 + 哪裡？`

> 你住在哪裡？  
> Where do you live?

**Common warning:** 哪裡 is common in Taiwan.

## 15. 怎麼

**Function:** ask how or why.

**Structure:** `Subject + 怎麼 + Verb?`

> 這個字怎麼念？  
> How do you pronounce this character?

**Common warning:** Context decides whether it means how or why.

## 16. 為什麼

**Function:** ask for a reason.

**Structure:** `Subject + 為什麼 + Predicate?`

> 你為什麼學中文？  
> Why are you learning Chinese?

**Common warning:** Use 怎麼 for a method; 為什麼 for a reason.

## 17. 的（possession）

**Function:** show possession.

**Structure:** `Owner + 的 + Noun`

> 這是我的雨傘。  
> This is my umbrella.

**Common warning:** 的 may be omitted with close family terms.

## 18. 的（description）

**Function:** modify a noun.

**Structure:** `Description + 的 + Noun`

> 我喜歡安靜的咖啡店。  
> I like quiet cafés.

**Common warning:** Put the full description before 的.

## 19. Number + measure word + noun

**Function:** count nouns.

**Structure:** `Number + MW + Noun`

> 我買了三本書。  
> I bought three books.

**Common warning:** Most counted nouns require a measure word.

## 20. 幾 / 多少

**Function:** ask quantity.

**Structure:** `幾 + MW + Noun? / 多少 + Noun?`

> 你有幾個兄弟姊妹？  
> How many siblings do you have?

**Common warning:** 幾 usually expects a relatively small number.

# 2. Time, Aspect, and Sequence

## 21. 了（completed action）

**Function:** mark a completed action.

**Structure:** `Subject + Verb + Object + 了`

> 我已經寄信了。  
> I have already sent the email.

**Common warning:** 了 is not simply past tense.

## 22. 了（new situation）

**Function:** show a changed situation.

**Structure:** `New situation + 了`

> 天黑了。  
> It has become dark.

**Common warning:** Sentence-final 了 often signals a new state.

## 23. 過

**Function:** describe past experience.

**Structure:** `Subject + Verb + 過 + Object`

> 我去過台南三次。  
> I have been to Tainan three times.

**Common warning:** 過 emphasizes experience rather than one event.

## 24. 著

**Function:** show a continuing state.

**Structure:** `Verb + 著`

> 門開著。  
> The door is open.

**Common warning:** 著 often describes a state resulting from an action.

## 25. 在 / 正在（progressive）

**Function:** show an action in progress.

**Structure:** `Subject + (正)在 + Verb`

> 我正在開會。  
> I am in a meeting.

**Common warning:** Do not use 正在 for simple location.

## 26. 已經…了

**Function:** show something has already happened.

**Structure:** `已經 + Predicate + 了`

> 我已經吃過了。  
> I have already eaten.

**Common warning:** Both words are common together, but not always required.

## 27. 還沒

**Function:** show something has not happened yet.

**Structure:** `還沒 + Verb`

> 我還沒決定。  
> I have not decided yet.

**Common warning:** Do not add 了 after an unfinished action.

## 28. 快要…了

**Function:** show something will happen soon.

**Structure:** `快要 + Verb + 了`

> 電影快要開始了。  
> The movie is about to start.

**Common warning:** The final 了 marks the approaching change.

## 29. 就要…了

**Function:** show an imminent expected event.

**Structure:** `就要 + Verb + 了`

> 我們下個月就要畢業了。  
> We are graduating next month.

**Common warning:** It often sounds more definite than 快要.

## 30. 剛

**Function:** show a very recent action.

**Structure:** `剛 + Verb`

> 我剛到公司。  
> I just arrived at the office.

**Common warning:** 剛 directly modifies the verb.

## 31. 剛才

**Function:** refer to the time just now.

**Structure:** `剛才 + Sentence`

> 剛才有人打給你。  
> Someone called you just now.

**Common warning:** 剛才 can function as a time noun.

## 32. 才

**Function:** express lateness or difficulty.

**Structure:** `Time/Condition + 才 + Verb`

> 他十一點才下班。  
> He did not finish work until eleven.

**Common warning:** It suggests later or harder than expected.

## 33. 就

**Function:** express earliness or immediacy.

**Structure:** `Time/Condition + 就 + Verb`

> 她六點就起床了。  
> She got up as early as six.

**Common warning:** It suggests earlier or easier than expected.

## 34. 再

**Function:** express future repetition.

**Structure:** `再 + Verb`

> 我們明天再談。  
> We will talk again tomorrow.

**Common warning:** Use mainly for future or intended repetition.

## 35. 又

**Function:** express past or current repetition.

**Structure:** `又 + Verb + 了`

> 他今天又遲到了。  
> He was late again today.

**Common warning:** Use when something has happened again.

## 36. 一直

**Function:** show continuity.

**Structure:** `一直 + Verb/Adjective`

> 昨晚一直下雨。  
> It rained continuously last night.

**Common warning:** It can describe an action or unchanged state.

## 37. 先…再…

**Function:** show ordered steps.

**Structure:** `先 + A，再 + B`

> 我們先吃飯，再看電影。  
> We will eat first and then watch a movie.

**Common warning:** Use 再 for the second step.

## 38. 一…就…

**Function:** show immediate sequence.

**Structure:** `一 + A + 就 + B`

> 我一到家就洗澡。  
> I shower as soon as I get home.

**Common warning:** The actions need a clear time relationship.

## 39. 從…到…

**Function:** show a range.

**Structure:** `從 + A + 到 + B`

> 我從九點工作到六點。  
> I work from nine to six.

**Common warning:** Use parallel endpoints.

## 40. 以前 / 以後

**Function:** express before or after.

**Structure:** `Event + 以前/以後`

> 下班以後，我去超市。  
> After work, I go to the supermarket.

**Common warning:** Put the reference event before 以前 or 以後.

# 3. Comparison, Degree, and Change

## 41. 比

**Function:** compare two things.

**Structure:** `A + 比 + B + Adjective`

> 台北比高雄冷。  
> Taipei is colder than Kaohsiung.

**Common warning:** Do not normally add 很 after the adjective.

## 42. 沒有…那麼…

**Function:** show A is less than B.

**Structure:** `A + 沒有 + B + 那麼 + Adj`

> 今天沒有昨天那麼熱。  
> Today is not as hot as yesterday.

**Common warning:** This is a comparison, not possession.

## 43. 跟…一樣

**Function:** show equality.

**Structure:** `A + 跟 + B + 一樣 + Adj`

> 這件跟那件一樣貴。  
> This one is as expensive as that one.

**Common warning:** Add the compared quality after 一樣.

## 44. 比較

**Function:** show relative preference or degree.

**Structure:** `比較 + Adj/Verb`

> 我比較喜歡安靜的地方。  
> I prefer quieter places.

**Common warning:** The other option may be unstated.

## 45. 更

**Function:** increase a degree.

**Structure:** `更 + Adj/Verb`

> 晚上會更冷。  
> It will be even colder at night.

**Common warning:** It usually builds on an existing comparison.

## 46. 最

**Function:** form a superlative.

**Structure:** `最 + Adj/Verb`

> 這是我最喜歡的餐廳。  
> This is my favorite restaurant.

**Common warning:** Place 最 directly before the predicate.

## 47. 越來越…

**Function:** show gradual change.

**Structure:** `越來越 + Adj/Verb`

> 我的中文越來越好了。  
> My Chinese is getting better.

**Common warning:** Do not insert 很 after 越來越.

## 48. 越…越…

**Function:** link two changing factors.

**Structure:** `越 + A + 越 + B`

> 這首歌越聽越好聽。  
> This song sounds better the more I hear it.

**Common warning:** Both parts should describe linked changes.

## 49. 變得

**Function:** show change into a new state.

**Structure:** `變得 + Adjective`

> 他變得比較開朗。  
> He became more outgoing.

**Common warning:** The result state follows 變得.

## 50. 太…了

**Function:** show excess or strong degree.

**Structure:** `太 + Adjective + 了`

> 房租太貴了。  
> The rent is too expensive.

**Common warning:** 太好了 can be positive: 'That is great.'

## 51. 有點

**Function:** show a small undesirable degree.

**Structure:** `有點 + Adjective`

> 今天有點累。  
> I am a little tired today.

**Common warning:** It often carries a mildly negative feeling.

## 52. 一點

**Function:** show a small quantity or adjustment.

**Structure:** `Adj + 一點 / 一點 + Noun`

> 可以說慢一點嗎？  
> Could you speak more slowly?

**Common warning:** Use it after an adjective for adjustment.

## 53. Verb + 得 + complement

**Function:** describe how an action is done.

**Structure:** `Verb + 得 + Adjective`

> 她中文說得很流利。  
> She speaks Chinese fluently.

**Common warning:** Repeat the verb when it already has an object.

## 54. 極了

**Function:** express an extreme degree.

**Structure:** `Adjective + 極了`

> 夜市熱鬧極了。  
> The night market is extremely lively.

**Common warning:** It is more emphatic than 很.

## 55. 差不多

**Function:** show approximation or similarity.

**Structure:** `A 跟 B 差不多 / 差不多 + Number`

> 我們的身高差不多。  
> We are about the same height.

**Common warning:** It can mean almost or approximately.

# 4. Logic and Connections

## 56. 因為…所以…

**Function:** connect cause and result.

**Structure:** `因為 + Cause，所以 + Result`

> 因為下雨，所以活動取消了。  
> The event was canceled because it rained.

**Common warning:** One half may be omitted when clear.

## 57. 所以

**Function:** introduce a result.

**Structure:** `Cause，所以 + Result`

> 捷運停駛了，所以我搭計程車。  
> The MRT stopped, so I took a taxi.

**Common warning:** It introduces the consequence.

## 58. 如果…就…

**Function:** express a neutral condition.

**Structure:** `如果 + Condition，就 + Result`

> 如果你有空，我們就吃飯。  
> If you are free, we will eat together.

**Common warning:** 就 may be omitted in casual speech.

## 59. 要是…就…

**Function:** express an informal condition.

**Structure:** `要是 + Condition，就 + Result`

> 要是下雨，我們就改天去。  
> If it rains, we will go another day.

**Common warning:** It is more conversational than 如果.

## 60. 只要…就…

**Function:** show a sufficient condition.

**Structure:** `只要 + Condition，就 + Result`

> 只要帶護照就可以。  
> As long as you bring your passport, it is fine.

**Common warning:** Do not confuse it with 只有…才….

## 61. 只有…才…

**Function:** show a necessary condition.

**Structure:** `只有 + Condition，才 + Result`

> 只有會員才能進去。  
> Only members can enter.

**Common warning:** The result requires the condition.

## 62. 除非…不然…

**Function:** express an exception.

**Structure:** `除非 + Exception，不然 + Result`

> 除非現在出發，不然會遲到。  
> Unless you leave now, you will be late.

**Common warning:** 不然 gives the default consequence.

## 63. 雖然…但是…

**Function:** express factual contrast.

**Structure:** `雖然 + Fact，但是 + Contrast`

> 雖然很累，但是她完成了。  
> Although tired, she finished it.

**Common warning:** 還是 can strengthen persistence in the second clause.

## 64. 即使…也…

**Function:** show an unchanged result under a condition.

**Structure:** `即使 + Condition，也 + Result`

> 即使下雨，我也會去。  
> Even if it rains, I will go.

**Common warning:** It is often hypothetical, unlike 雖然.

## 65. 不但…而且…

**Function:** add a stronger second point.

**Structure:** `不但 + A，而且 + B`

> 他不但會中文，而且會日文。  
> He speaks not only Chinese but also Japanese.

**Common warning:** Keep both parts grammatically parallel.

## 66. 除了…以外，也…

**Function:** add another item.

**Structure:** `除了 + A + 以外，也 + B`

> 除了咖啡以外，也賣茶。  
> Besides coffee, they also sell tea.

**Common warning:** This means besides, not except.

## 67. 除了…以外，都…

**Function:** exclude one item.

**Structure:** `除了 + A + 以外，都 + Result`

> 除了小王以外，大家都到了。  
> Everyone arrived except Xiao Wang.

**Common warning:** 都 marks the remaining group.

## 68. 既然…就…

**Function:** draw a decision from an accepted fact.

**Structure:** `既然 + Fact，就 + Decision`

> 既然來了，就一起吃吧。  
> Since you are here, let us eat together.

**Common warning:** The first fact is already accepted.

## 69. 不過

**Function:** introduce a mild limitation.

**Structure:** `Statement，不過 + Contrast`

> 這間店很好吃，不過有點貴。  
> This shop is delicious but a little expensive.

**Common warning:** It is often softer than 但是.

## 70. 還是（despite that）

**Function:** show persistence despite circumstances.

**Structure:** `Contrast + 還是 + Result`

> 雖然很晚，我還是去了。  
> Although it was late, I still went.

**Common warning:** This is not the alternative-question 還是.

# 5. Objects, Complements, and Direction

## 71. 把

**Function:** highlight how an object is handled.

**Structure:** `Subject + 把 + Object + Verb + Result`

> 請把門關上。  
> Please close the door.

**Common warning:** The object should be specific and the verb needs an outcome.

## 72. 被

**Function:** form a passive sentence.

**Structure:** `Receiver + 被 + Agent + Verb`

> 我的腳踏車被偷了。  
> My bicycle was stolen.

**Common warning:** The agent may be omitted.

## 73. 讓

**Function:** cause or allow.

**Structure:** `Subject + 讓 + Person + Verb`

> 這件事讓我很擔心。  
> This matter makes me worried.

**Common warning:** It can mean let or make.

## 74. 叫

**Function:** tell someone to do something.

**Structure:** `Subject + 叫 + Person + Verb`

> 老師叫我們明天交作業。  
> The teacher told us to submit it tomorrow.

**Common warning:** It is direct and conversational.

## 75. 給

**Function:** mark a recipient.

**Structure:** `Subject + 給 + Person + Object`

> 我給他一張名片。  
> I gave him a business card.

**Common warning:** Word order varies with different verbs.

## 76. 幫

**Function:** do something for someone.

**Structure:** `幫 + Person + Verb`

> 可以幫我看一下嗎？  
> Could you take a look for me?

**Common warning:** The beneficiary comes before the action.

## 77. 對

**Function:** show the target of an attitude.

**Structure:** `對 + Target + Predicate`

> 他對這份工作有興趣。  
> He is interested in this job.

**Common warning:** The target follows 對.

## 78. 跟

**Function:** show accompaniment or interaction.

**Structure:** `跟 + Person + Verb`

> 我要跟客戶開會。  
> I have a meeting with a client.

**Common warning:** It can also connect nouns as and.

## 79. 向

**Function:** show direction toward a target.

**Structure:** `向 + Target + Verb`

> 你可以向櫃台詢問。  
> You can ask at the counter.

**Common warning:** Common with 詢問、申請、道歉.

## 80. 離

**Function:** express distance.

**Structure:** `A + 離 + B + Distance`

> 我家離捷運站很近。  
> My home is near the MRT station.

**Common warning:** The reference point follows 離.

## 81. Verb + 完

**Function:** show completion.

**Structure:** `Verb + 完 + Object`

> 我還沒看完報告。  
> I have not finished the report.

**Common warning:** 完 follows the verb directly.

## 82. Verb + 到

**Function:** show successful attainment.

**Structure:** `Verb + 到 + Object/Result`

> 我終於找到工作了。  
> I finally found a job.

**Common warning:** It shows the intended result was reached.

## 83. Verb + 懂

**Function:** show successful understanding.

**Structure:** `聽/看 + 懂`

> 你聽得懂嗎？  
> Can you understand it?

**Common warning:** Use 聽懂 for speech and 看懂 for text or images.

## 84. Verb + 見

**Function:** show successful perception.

**Structure:** `看/聽 + 見`

> 我沒聽見你叫我。  
> I did not hear you call me.

**Common warning:** It emphasizes perception, not deliberate attention.

## 85. Verb + 好

**Function:** show completion and readiness.

**Structure:** `Verb + 好 + Object`

> 我已經訂好位子了。  
> I have reserved the seats.

**Common warning:** It implies the result is ready to use.

## 86. 來 / 去（direction）

**Function:** show motion toward or away from the speaker.

**Structure:** `Verb + 來/去`

> 請拿一杯水過來。  
> Please bring a glass of water here.

**Common warning:** Use 來 toward the speaker and 去 away.

## 87. 上來 / 上去

**Function:** show upward movement.

**Structure:** `Verb + 上來/上去`

> 你可以走上來嗎？  
> Can you walk up here?

**Common warning:** Choose 來 or 去 from the speaker's viewpoint.

## 88. 進來 / 進去

**Function:** show inward movement.

**Structure:** `Verb + 進來/進去`

> 外面很冷，快進來。  
> It is cold outside; come in.

**Common warning:** 進來 moves toward the speaker.

## 89. 出來 / 出去

**Function:** show outward movement.

**Structure:** `Verb + 出來/出去`

> 請把垃圾拿出去。  
> Please take the trash outside.

**Common warning:** 出去 moves away from the speaker.

## 90. 起來

**Function:** show rising, beginning, or an impression.

**Structure:** `Verb + 起來`

> 這個方法用起來很方便。  
> This method is convenient to use.

**Common warning:** Its meaning depends strongly on context.

# 6. Ability, Desire, and Obligation

## 91. 會（learned ability）

**Function:** express a learned skill.

**Structure:** `會 + Verb`

> 我會騎機車。  
> I know how to ride a scooter.

**Common warning:** Use 能 for circumstantial ability.

## 92. 會（probability）

**Function:** express a likely event.

**Structure:** `會 + Verb/Adjective`

> 明天會下雨。  
> It will rain tomorrow.

**Common warning:** Here it does not mean learned ability.

## 93. 能

**Function:** express circumstantial ability.

**Structure:** `能 + Verb`

> 我今天不能加班。  
> I cannot work overtime today.

**Common warning:** It often depends on time, health, or rules.

## 94. 可以

**Function:** express permission or acceptability.

**Structure:** `可以 + Verb`

> 這裡可以拍照嗎？  
> May I take pictures here?

**Common warning:** It commonly asks or grants permission.

## 95. 想

**Function:** express a mild wish or intention.

**Structure:** `想 + Verb/Noun`

> 我想換工作。  
> I am thinking about changing jobs.

**Common warning:** It is softer than 要.

## 96. 要

**Function:** express strong intention, need, or future.

**Structure:** `要 + Verb/Noun`

> 我要一杯無糖紅茶。  
> I want an unsweetened black tea.

**Common warning:** Context decides the exact meaning.

## 97. 需要

**Function:** express necessity.

**Structure:** `需要 + Noun/Verb`

> 申請時需要帶護照。  
> You need a passport when applying.

**Common warning:** It is necessity, not merely desire.

## 98. 應該

**Function:** express advice or expectation.

**Structure:** `應該 + Verb`

> 你應該先休息。  
> You should rest first.

**Common warning:** It can also mean probably.

## 99. 一定

**Function:** express certainty.

**Structure:** `一定 + Verb/Adjective`

> 我一定會準時到。  
> I will definitely arrive on time.

**Common warning:** It is stronger than 應該.

## 100. 可能

**Function:** express possibility.

**Structure:** `可能 + 會 + Verb`

> 他可能會晚一點到。  
> He may arrive a little late.

**Common warning:** 會 is common after 可能.

## 101. 最好

**Function:** give strong practical advice.

**Structure:** `最好 + Verb`

> 你最好先預約。  
> You had better reserve first.

**Common warning:** It can sound stronger than 應該.

## 102. 必須

**Function:** express a strict requirement.

**Structure:** `必須 + Verb`

> 進入前必須出示證件。  
> You must show identification before entering.

**Common warning:** It is formal and strong.

## 103. 得（must）

**Function:** express conversational necessity.

**Structure:** `得 + Verb`

> 我得先走了。  
> I have to leave first.

**Common warning:** This 得 is pronounced děi.

## 104. 不要

**Function:** form a negative command.

**Structure:** `不要 + Verb`

> 不要忘記帶雨傘。  
> Do not forget your umbrella.

**Common warning:** It tells someone not to do something.

## 105. 別

**Function:** form a concise negative command.

**Structure:** `別 + Verb + 了`

> 別擔心了。  
> Do not worry.

**Common warning:** It is common and conversational.

# 7. Natural Conversation

## 106. 是不是

**Function:** ask for confirmation.

**Structure:** `是不是 + Predicate?`

> 你是不是換手機了？  
> Did you change your phone?

**Common warning:** It often suggests the speaker has a guess.

## 107. 有沒有

**Function:** ask whether something exists or happened.

**Structure:** `有沒有 + Noun/Verb?`

> 你有沒有收到我的信？  
> Did you receive my email?

**Common warning:** It is common for completed-action questions.

## 108. 要不要

**Function:** offer or ask willingness.

**Structure:** `要不要 + Verb/Noun?`

> 你要不要一起去？  
> Do you want to go together?

**Common warning:** It is more conversational than 嗎.

## 109. 可不可以

**Function:** ask permission.

**Structure:** `可不可以 + Verb?`

> 這裡可不可以刷卡？  
> Can I pay by card here?

**Common warning:** It focuses on permission or acceptability.

## 110. 能不能

**Function:** ask practical possibility.

**Structure:** `能不能 + Verb?`

> 你明天能不能來？  
> Can you come tomorrow?

**Common warning:** It focuses on ability or circumstances.

## 111. 怎麼了

**Function:** ask what happened.

**Structure:** `Subject + 怎麼了?`

> 你看起來不舒服，怎麼了？  
> You look unwell. What happened?

**Common warning:** It asks about a changed situation.

## 112. 怎麼辦

**Function:** ask what should be done.

**Structure:** `Situation，怎麼辦？`

> 我忘了帶錢，怎麼辦？  
> I forgot my money. What should I do?

**Common warning:** It asks for a solution.

## 113. 看起來

**Function:** describe a visual impression.

**Structure:** `看起來 + Adjective`

> 這道菜看起來很好吃。  
> This dish looks delicious.

**Common warning:** The judgment comes from appearance.

## 114. 聽起來

**Function:** describe an impression from sound or information.

**Structure:** `聽起來 + Adjective`

> 這個計畫聽起來不錯。  
> This plan sounds good.

**Common warning:** It can refer to sound or an idea.

## 115. 原來

**Function:** show a new realization.

**Structure:** `原來 + Sentence`

> 原來你也住台北。  
> So you also live in Taipei.

**Common warning:** It often corrects an earlier assumption.

## 116. 難怪

**Function:** show an understandable result.

**Structure:** `難怪 + Result`

> 難怪他沒來，原來他生病了。  
> No wonder he did not come; he was sick.

**Common warning:** It introduces the explained result.

## 117. 沒想到

**Function:** express an unexpected result.

**Structure:** `沒想到 + Sentence`

> 沒想到今天這麼多人。  
> I did not expect so many people today.

**Common warning:** It signals surprise.

## 118. 不一定

**Function:** show uncertainty or no fixed rule.

**Structure:** `不一定 + Predicate`

> 貴的不一定比較好。  
> Expensive things are not necessarily better.

**Common warning:** It means not necessarily.

## 119. 看情況

**Function:** leave a decision dependent on circumstances.

**Structure:** `到時候 + 看情況`

> 週末要不要去，到時候看情況。  
> We will decide this weekend based on the situation.

**Common warning:** It is natural spoken Chinese for an undecided plan.

## 120. 再說

**Function:** postpone discussion or add a reason.

**Structure:** `Future time + 再說`

> 這件事明天再說吧。  
> Let us discuss this tomorrow.

**Common warning:** It may also mean besides.

## 121. 還是 / 或者

**Function:** distinguish alternatives.

**Structure:** `Question: A 還是 B? Statement: A 或者 B`

> 你想喝茶還是咖啡？  
> Would you like tea or coffee?

**Common warning:** Use 還是 in direct alternatives and 或者 in statements.

## 122. 一邊…一邊…

**Function:** show simultaneous actions.

**Structure:** `一邊 + A，一邊 + B`

> 她一邊走路，一邊講電話。  
> She talks on the phone while walking.

**Common warning:** The same subject normally performs both actions.

## 123. 又…又…

**Function:** show two qualities or actions.

**Structure:** `又 + A + 又 + B`

> 這間房子又大又明亮。  
> This house is both large and bright.

**Common warning:** Use parallel forms in both positions.

## 124. 連…都…

**Function:** emphasize a surprising extreme.

**Structure:** `連 + Item + 都 + Predicate`

> 這題連老師都不知道。  
> Even the teacher does not know this.

**Common warning:** The item after 連 should be surprising.

## 125. 是…的

**Function:** emphasize details of a known event.

**Structure:** `是 + Detail + Verb + 的`

> 我是去年來台灣的。  
> It was last year that I came to Taiwan.

**Common warning:** Use it for when, where, how, or by whom.

# Quick comparisons

| Pair | Main difference |
|---|---|
| 才 / 就 | 才 suggests later or harder than expected; 就 suggests earlier or easier than expected. |
| 再 / 又 | 再 usually refers to future repetition; 又 refers to past, current, or habitual repetition. |
| 不 / 沒有 | 不 negates habits, states, and future actions; 沒有 negates possession or completed actions. |
| 會 / 能 / 可以 | 會 is learned ability or probability; 能 is circumstantial ability; 可以 is permission. |
| 想 / 要 / 需要 | 想 is a mild wish; 要 is a stronger intention; 需要 is necessity. |
| 了 / 過 / 著 | 了 marks completion/change; 過 marks experience; 著 marks a continuing state. |
| 越來越 / 越…越… | 越來越 shows gradual change; 越…越… links two changes. |
| 有點 / 一點 | 有點 comes before a usually undesirable adjective; 一點 follows an adjective for adjustment or means a small amount. |
| 還是 / 或者 | 還是 is used in direct alternative questions; 或者 is used in statements. |
| 只要…就… / 只有…才… | The first marks a sufficient condition; the second marks a necessary condition. |

intro: The phrases that make you sound like a person rather than a textbook. Taiwanese

# Suggested learning order

- **Stage 1:** Patterns 1–40 — sentence building, questions, time, and aspect.
- **Stage 2:** Patterns 41–75 — comparison, logic, objects, complements, and direction.
- **Stage 3:** Patterns 76–125 — ability, intention, and natural conversation.
